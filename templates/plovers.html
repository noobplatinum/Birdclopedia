Plovers

Charadriidae

Description

Plovers are small, agile shorebirds known for their quick movements and distinctive calls. They forage on the ground, pecking at insects and small crustaceans, and their short bills make them well-suited for this diet. Plovers are ground-nesting birds, often using open and exposed areas, and rely on their excellent camouflage to protect their eggs and chicks from predators.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Charadrius vociferus
  • Length: 20-28 cm
  • Weight: 75-125 gr
  • Coloration: Brown back, white underside, and distinctive black bands across the chest and head, with orange tones on the rump during flight
  • Habitat: Coastal beaches, mudflats, grasslands, and even urban areas
  • Defining Feature: Short bills and long legs, paired with their distinctive "broken-wing" display to distract predators

Fun Facts

Fact 1

The Killdeer, a type of plover, is famous for its "broken-wing" act, where it pretends to be injured to lure predators away from its nest.

Fact 2

Some plovers, like the Pacific Golden Plover, are remarkable navigators, flying thousands of kilometers non-stop across oceans during their migrations.
