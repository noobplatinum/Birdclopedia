Ducks

Anatidae

Description

Ducks are waterfowl that are highly adaptable to both freshwater and saltwater habitats. Known for their playful swimming and unique quacking sounds, they use their broad bills to filter food like aquatic plants, insects, and small fish from water. Ducks are social birds, often seen in flocks, and their migratory patterns span thousands of kilometers during seasonal changes.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Anas platyrhynchos
  • Length: 50-65 cm
  • Weight: 0.7-1.6 kg
  • Coloration: Often a mix of iridescent greens, browns, and whites, with males being more vibrant
  • Habitat: Wetlands, ponds, rivers, lakes, and coastal regions
  • Defining Feature: Webbed feet for swimming and a broad, flat bill for foraging

Fun Facts

Fact 1

Ducks have waterproof feathers due to a special oil produced by a gland near their tails, which they spread during preening.

Fact 2

Some duck species, like the Harlequin Duck, can thrive in fast-moving mountain streams and are known to dive up to 20 meters underwater to forage.
