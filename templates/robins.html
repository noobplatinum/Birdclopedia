Robins

Turdidae

Description

Robins are iconic songbirds associated with the arrival of spring in many regions. Known for their melodious tunes, they are early risers and can often be seen pulling worms from the ground. Robins are highly adaptable and thrive in both natural and urban environments. Their friendly demeanor and vibrant plumage make them beloved backyard visitors.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Turdus migratorius
  • Length: 23-28 cm
  • Weight: 77-85 grams
  • Coloration: Grayish-brown back, bright orange or reddish chest, and white underparts with a yellowish bill
  • Habitat: Forests, gardens, parks, and open fields, often near human settlements
  • Defining Feature: Cheerful song and distinctive orange-red breast

Fun Facts

Fact 1

Robins are known for their territorial nature and will aggressively defend their nests from intruders, even attacking their reflection in mirrors or windows.
