Sandpipers

Scolopacidae

Description

Sandpipers are small to medium-sized wading birds known for their quick, darting movements along shorelines. They feed on small invertebrates, probing the mud or sand with their sensitive bills. Sandpipers are migratory birds, often traveling vast distances between breeding and wintering grounds. Their intricate and synchronized flock movements during flight are a spectacular sight.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Calidris alpina
  • Length: 17-21 cm
  • Weight: 40-70 gr
  • Coloration: Mottled brown and gray upperparts with white underparts, black belly patch during breeding season
  • Habitat: Coastal mudflats, sandy beaches, wetlands, and tundra
  • Defining Feature: Long, slender legs and a slightly curved bill adapted for probing soft ground

Fun Facts

Fact 1

Some sandpipers, like the Red Knot, undertake one of the longest migrations in the bird world, traveling over 15,000 kilometers annually.
