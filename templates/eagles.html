Eagles

Accipitridae

Description

Eagles are large apex predators, with heavy heads and beaks. They are known for their strength and keen eyesight. Eagles catch preys by swooping down from great heights and using their powerful talons to grab them. Their beaks are as hard as bone!

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Aquila chrysaetos
  • Length: 70-90 cm
  • Weight: 3-6 kg
  • Coloration: Dark brown with lighter golden-brown plumage
  • Habitat: Mountains, forests, and open plains
  • Defining Feature: Powerful build and keen eyesight

Fun Facts

Fact 1

Some species of eagles, like the Bald Eagle, can live for over 30 years in the wild.

Fact 2

Eagle nests are often called "eyries" and can be enormous, sometimes reaching 10 feet in diameter and weighing over a ton!
