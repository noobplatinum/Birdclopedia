Shorebirds

Birds Between Land and Sea

Overview

Shorebirds are an unique group of birds that are found in habitats such as beaches and mudflats.
They are known for their long limbs and beaks, which helps them forage for food in sand.
They are also known for their distinctive calls, which they use to communicate with and warn others.
Shorebirds are found on every continent except Antarctica, and they play an important role in the ecosystem by controlling insect populations and aerating the soil.
