Hummingbirds

Trochilidae

Description

Hummingbirds are tiny, dazzling birds renowned for their vibrant plumage and incredible flying abilities. Using their long, slender bills and specialized tongues, they feed on nectar from flowers, playing a critical role in pollination. Their high metabolism requires them to eat almost constantly, consuming twice their body weight in food daily. With their ability to hover like helicopters, hummingbirds are a marvel of nature's engineering.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Archilochus colubris
  • Length: 7-13 cm
  • Weight: 2-5 gr
  • Coloration: Iridescent green and red or ruby tones, often with shimmering metallic hues
  • Habitat: Tropical and temperate regions, including forests, gardens, and meadows
  • Defining Feature: Rapid wing beats (50-80 beats per second) enabling hovering and backward flight
