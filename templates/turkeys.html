Turkeys

Phasianidae

Description

Turkeys are large, social birds known for their striking appearance and behavior. Wild Turkeys are agile on the ground, capable of running at speeds up to 20 mph, and can fly short distances at speeds of up to 55 mph. They are foragers, feeding on a mix of nuts, seeds, insects, and small reptiles. Males, known as toms, display elaborate courtship dances and gobbles to attract females, or hens.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Meleagris gallopavo
  • Length: 100-125 cm
  • Weight: 5-11 kg
  • Coloration: Iridescent bronze and green body feathers with a bare, colorful head that can change shades of red, blue, and white depending on mood
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and agricultural fields, especially in North America
  • Defining Feature: Large size, fan-shaped tail, and a distinctive fleshy "snood" hanging over the beak

Fun Facts

Fact 1

The Wild Turkey was once considered for the national bird of the United States, as suggested by Benjamin Franklin, who admired its resourcefulness and character.

Fact 2

Turkeys have excellent vision, with a field of view of about 270 degrees, and can see in color, making them difficult to sneak up on.
