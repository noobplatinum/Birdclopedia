Owls

Strigidae

Description

Owls are nocturnal birds of prey, known for their distinct facial disks and forward-facing eyes that allow for excellent depth perception. With powerful talons, sharp beaks, and extraordinary hearing, they are adept at hunting small mammals, birds, and insects. Their ability to turn their heads up to 270 degrees adds to their mystique, making them symbols of wisdom and stealth in many cultures.

Specifications

  • Scientific Name: Tyto alba
  • Length: 25-40 cm
  • Weight: 0.4-1.5 kg
  • Coloration: Varied; often mottled brown, white, and gray for camouflage
  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands, deserts, and urban areas
  • Defining Feature: Silent flight due to specialized feather structures and exceptional night vision

Fun Facts

Fact 1

Some owl species can hear prey under layers of snow, pinpointing movement solely through sound.

Fact 2

Owls have a unique adaptation called “zygodactyl feet,” with two toes pointing forward and two backward, providing a better grip when catching prey.
